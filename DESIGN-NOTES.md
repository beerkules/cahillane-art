# Design Notes

Living scratchpad for the CAHILLANE site. Not published — reference only.

## Mobile inspiration

### fabianutta.com
Fine-art portfolio + shop. Things worth borrowing for mobile:
- **Image-first, single column.** Artwork is full-width; text is minimal and sits *below* the image, never on top of it. Paintings are the focal point.
- **Hero carousel** of featured works at the top, auto-rotating, minimal text overlay — "visual discovery" before any reading.
- **Dual images per work** (studio shot + installation/in-situ shot) to give scale and context.
- **Collapsed nav** into a hamburger; logo centered. Secondary stuff (language DE/EN, currency) tucked into dropdowns so the bar stays clean.
- **Rhythmic section headers** ("New Original Paintings available") to break the scroll.
- **Newsletter signup placed mid-page**, not just the footer.

### mmcgrath.com
Minimalist artist portfolio + shop. Worth borrowing:
- **Stark, high-contrast, near-monochrome** — almost no decoration, gallery-like.
- **Slide-out menu** with a tight set: Select Works, Shop (Prints / Original Drawings), About, Mailing List. Curated sections rather than one infinite feed.
- **Cart with item count + search** in the header — commerce is one tap away but visually quiet.
- **Footer does work**: recent exhibitions listed (positions the artist), Instagram link, copyright.

## Common thread (our direction)
- Let the work carry it; keep type quiet and let images go full-bleed.
- Curated sections > infinite scroll.
- Email capture should appear more than once (hero + mid-page), the way it already does on the coming-soon page.
- Keep the dark, restrained palette we have — it matches mmcgrath's gallery feel.

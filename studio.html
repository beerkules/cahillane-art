Studio

Each work is its own reality — a place that might or might not exist, built from genuine metal, layered light and lines drawn by hand.

A CAHILLANE painting is not an image of a place but a coordinate: one exact location, at one exact moment, in a dimension parallel to ours. The luminosity is not painted — it is reflected. Genuine precious metal lies beneath every square centimetre; light passes through the translucent colour, strikes the metal, and returns. The painting is lit from within, and it shifts as you move before it.

01 — Silver

Each work begins as a mirror. A large panel is laid, sheet by sheet, with genuine silver leaf — or, for the warm works, gold. This metal ground is the light source of the entire painting.

02 — Glazes

Over the metal, translucent pigment glazes are layered — twenty, thirty passes — until the surface darkens toward night. Where few layers lie, the metal glows through like distant light; where many lie, the world sinks into depth.

03 — Engraving

Then the structure appears. Straight lines, engraved by hand through the colour down to the metal, build the wireframe of a world — its planes, depths and vanishing points. Some lines end mid-way; some run past the edge, as if the world continued beyond the panel.

04 — Seal

A reversible conservation varnish protects the surface. Each work is marked, and sold with a signed certificate of authenticity documenting its materials and the precious metal used.

Materials

Genuine 999 silver, 23.75 kt gold and palladium leaf. Lightfast mineral pigments — at times lapis lazuli, diamond dust or meteorite ground into the deepest zones. Built on aluminium, to stay perfectly flat for generations.
